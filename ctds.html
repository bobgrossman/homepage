The Center for Translational Data Science (CTDS) at the University of Chicago is developing the discipline of data science and its applications to problems in biology, medicine, healthcare and the environment.

Today, CTDS develops and operate large scale data platforms to support research in cancer, cardiovascular disease, inflammatory bowel disease (IBD), Veterans’ health, pain management, and opioid use disorder, and environmental science.

CTDS researchers are also developing new machine learning and AI algorithms over the data in the platforms it operates.

CTDS has developed a number of important “firsts:” including, one of the first large scale data clouds (the NSF supported Open Science Data Cloud (2010-2016)); the first data cloud designed to host biomedical data and approved as a NIH Trusted Partner (the Bionimbus Protected Data Cloud (2013-2020)); the first large scale data commons (the NCI Genomic Data Commons (2016-present)); and, one of the first data meshes for biomedical data (The Biomedical Research Hub (2021-present).

CTDS operated data commons include:

  • NCI Genomic Data Commons
  • BloodPAC Data Commons
  • NIDDK IBD Genetics Consortium Data Commons
